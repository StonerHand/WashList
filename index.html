How matching works

Honest matching.
No silent deletes

Same artist isn't a duplicate. Different songs by the same artist stay separate. Different versions of one track are surfaced — but never auto-removed. You decide

/ 01 · Fingerprint

Normalize, then signal

Each track is normalized — case, punctuation, featured-artist tags, parenthetical "Remastered 2019" — then converted into a multi-axis fingerprint: ISRC, normalized title, artist set, duration, and metadata cluster

/ 02 · Confidence

Confidence, not certainty

Every match gets a 0–100 score. Above 92 → exact. 75–92 → probable. 50–75 → possible. Below 50 → related version, not duplicate. You see the score and the reason

/ 03 · Safety

What WashList never does

Same-artist isn't a match. Different songs by the same artist stay independent. Remixes and live versions stay visible as "related" — never auto-deleted. Nothing leaves your library without you
